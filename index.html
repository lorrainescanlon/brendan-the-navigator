Brendan
The
Navigator

484 AD

Brendan the Navigator was born in Fenit, Co.Kerry on the south west coast of Ireland in 484 AD. Brendan was a monk with a passion for travelling on the sea. He was an adventurer and fearlessly navigated the seas in handmade wooden framed boats called currachs, with no lighthouses or modern navigation equipment.

512 AD

In 512 AD Brendan set out on his legendary 7 year voyage in search of the isle of the blessed. It is believed that his journey took him from Brandon Creek in West Kerry northward across the Atlantic reaching Newfoundland and America. A Latin manuscript called Navigatio Sancti Brendani Abbatis details this epic journey.

578 AD

On his return to Ireland Brendan continued setting up monasteries, he died in 578 at Annaghdown in Co. Galway. Brendan is patron saint of mariners, sailors, whales and the US Navy. St Brendan's feast day is celebrated on May 16th marked with a pilgrimage on Mount Brandon on the Dingle Peninsula. St Brendan's festival takes place in Fenit annualy.

1976

In May 1976 almost 1400 years later, Tim Severin a British explorer set out to prove that Brendan’s voyage was indeed possible. He constructed a replica of Brendan’s boat and with a crew of 3 others set sail from Brandon Creek to follow the same route St. Brendan had taken in 512 AD. 13 months later Tim and his crew arrived in Newfoundland.
